I AM HUMAN

SOCIAL ACTIVISM CAMPAIGN

  • PROJECT SUMMARY

    This social activism campaign was created for the Human Rights Action Center (HRAC) as a way to educate the youth about their basic human rights. The task to create an ad campagin that would capture viewer's attention on the streets with its demanding appeal.


    TARGET AUDIENCE

    Youth audience between 14 and late 20s years of age were considered to be a majority that are likely to not know all of their human rights.

ABOUT

Hello friend, It’s me Garrett Schmohr. I am a young graphic designer located in the thriving city of Lincoln, NE. Since childhood I have had a strong passion for the arts. My creativity has lead me to spend countless hours drawing some comic strip, short stories that lead to an advantage in later years with essays. With collections of short home videos of my favorite movies with the settings be crafted out of cardboard boxes. In-between middle school and high school I stumbled into coding. Which I found an immense amount of joy in just seeing lines of code on a screen. While in high school I took a chance with a media arts class, mainly for the reason that we would learn how to code with html, css and a understanding in reading javascript and basic knowledge of how to use. But before we could get to that we learned composer in photography, making movie posters in photoshop and animations with flash. See that I had just as much fun doing these assignments as I did when I was geeking out with coding, I left that class with a large awareness of what career path I wanted to follow. When It came to picking a college to attend I look for ones that fit well with my needs and desires in experience. Southeast Community College jumped to the top of the list when they had a strong focus in web design. As usual I applied to the waiting list for the Graphic Design | Media Arts program at SCC due to offering web focused classes. As I was immersed in the design world I began to find interests other than web, but a strong respect for typography; and I picked up illustrating projects that had a function to their creation. As for who I am personally, I would describe myself as a funny, clever, individual, or a mystery box because you never know what’s going to happen during our interaction.
